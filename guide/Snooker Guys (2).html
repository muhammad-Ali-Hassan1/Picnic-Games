How to Play Snooker:

  1. Breaking Off:The game begins with a "break-off," where the player strikes the cue ball to hit the reds. A legal break-off requires that the cue ball must hit a red ball first.
  2. Potting Balls:After hitting a red ball, the player must pot one of the colored balls. When a colored ball is potted, it is returned to the table until all reds are cleared. After all reds are potted, the colors are potted in their designated order.
  3. Fouls:A player commits a foul if they fail to hit a ball correctly or cause the cue ball to land in a pocket. Fouls result in a penalty, and the opponent is awarded points, often allowing them a good chance to score.
