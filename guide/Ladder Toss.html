Ladder Toss

Ladder Toss (also known as Ladder Golf) is a popular outdoor game where players throw bolas (two balls connected by a string) at a three-rung ladder structure, aiming to hook the bolas onto one of the rungs for points. The game can be played by individuals or teams and is great for parties, picnics, tailgates, and family gatherings.

How to Play Ladder Toss:

  1. Setup:Set up the ladder structure at a reasonable distance between the two teams or players. Players or teams stand at the throwing line, which is the designated spot from where they will throw their bolas.
  2. Gameplay: Players take turns throwing bolas at the ladder, with the goal of landing them on one of the three rungs. Players typically throw one bola per turn, alternating between teammates or opponents. After all players have taken their turns, the round ends, and points are scored.
  3. Scoring: Points are awarded based on where the bolas land. A bola that hooks onto the top rung earns 3 points, the middle rung earns 2 points and the bottom rung earns 1 point. If a bola is knocked off or fails to land on a rung, it scores no points.
  4. Cancelling Points: In ladder toss, the points are calculated using a "cancelation" rule, meaning that the points scored by one team or player in a round can be canceled out by the opposing team or player.
  5. Winning the Game:The game is typically played to 21 points. A player or team must score exactly 21 points to win. If they exceed 21 points, they are "busted" and must go back to a lower score, such as 15 points, and continue from there.
