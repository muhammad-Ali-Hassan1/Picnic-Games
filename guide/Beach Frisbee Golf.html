Beach Frisbee Golf

Beach Frisbee Golf, also known as Disc Golf, is an exciting variation of traditional frisbee golf that combines the precision of golf with the fun of throwing a frisbee. Played on a beach or other outdoor sandy area, the game offers a relaxing yet competitive challenge.

How to Play Beach Frisbee Golf:

  1. Set Up: The game is typically played on a sandy beach or open outdoor area with enough space to create a course. If you're playing on the beach, you can use the natural landscape, such as trees, sand dunes, or other landmarks, as targets.
  2. Objective of the Game: The goal is to throw the frisbee from a starting point (usually called the tee) towards each target, with the aim of reaching the target in the fewest number of throws. Players take turns throwing their frisbee towards the target.
  3. Start of Play: Each hole starts at a tee-off point. The first player throws their frisbee, and the other players take their turns based on where the frisbee lands.
  4. Throwing the Frisbee: Players can throw the frisbee using various techniques, such as a backhand, forehand, or overhand throw.
  5. Scoring: Similar to traditional golf, each throw is counted as a stroke. The player who gets the frisbee to the target in the fewest number of throws wins the hole.
  6. Winning the Game: The player with the fewest total throws to reach the final target across all holes wins the game. The game can be played with multiple rounds or holes, such as 9 or 18, or it can be shortened for quicker play. If playing in teams, the team with the fewest combined throws wins.

Equipment Required for Beach Frisbee Golf:

To play Beach Frisbee Golf, you will need the following essential equipment:

  1. Frisbees (Discs)
  2. Hula hoops or buckets
  3. Cones or flags
  4. Natural obstacles
  5. Markers or Tee Areas
  6. Scorecard
  7. Water
  8. Bag or Storage
