Team Building

The Paper Tower Game is a popular team-building exercise designed to encourage creativity, collaboration, problem-solving, and communication within a group. It's an engaging and fun activity that challenges teams to work together under time constraints to build the tallest, most stable tower possible using only a few simple materials.

How to Play Team-Building Paper Tower:

  1. Divide Into Teams: The group is divided into smaller teams, typically of 3-5 people per team. The ideal number of teams depends on the total number of participants.
  2. Set the Rules: Each team is given an equal amount of paper, tape, and other materials (if allowed). Teams must build the tallest tower they can, but it must be free-standing (no external support like leaning against walls or using furniture). The tower must be built within a set time frame (usually 10-20 minutes).
  3. Time the Challenge: Start the timer for the designated time limit, giving teams enough time to strategize and build their towers. Teams should work together, discussing the design and methods to make the tower both tall and stable.
  4. Completion: Once the time is up, each team must stop building. The height of each tower is then measured, and stability is assessed. The tower must stand independently without any external support.
  5. Judging: The team with the tallest and most stable tower wins. Some variations of the game may also award points for creativity or team collaboration, with teams explaining their design process and strategy.

Equipment Required for Team-Building Paper Tower:

For the Team Building Paper Tower Game, the required equipment is minimal and simple, making it an easy-to-set-up and engaging activity. Here's a list of the essential items needed for this game:

  1. Paper
  2. Tape
  3. Scissors (Optional)
  4. Timer/Stopwatch
  5. Ruler or Measuring Tape
  6. Flat Surface/Table
