Croquet

Croquet is a lawn game that involves players hitting balls through a series of wicketٖs (also called hoops) using a mallet. It’s typically played on a rectangular grass or turf court. Here's how to play the game:

How to Play Croquet:

  1. Objective of the Game:The goal of croquet is to be the first player or team to get their balls through all the wickets in a specific order and then hit the "rover"ٖ stake or peg. The game can be played by 2 to 6 players.
  2. Basic Rules:Players decide on the order of play.Each player or team starts with a ball in a specific starting position near the first wicket.
  3. Additional Rules:f you hit your ball in an incorrect direction, fail to strike it properly, or make a mistake with your turn, you may be penalized.
  4. Gameplay:Players take turns hitting their ball with the mallet. The player must hit the ball with the mallet in such a way that it goes through the wickets in the correct order.
  5. Winning the Game:The first player (or team) to hit the final peg after going through all the wickets in the correct order wins the game.

Equipment Required for Bowling in the Backyard

To play a game of croquet, you will need the following equipmentThese items form the basic set of equipment required to play croquet.

  1. Croquet Mallets
  2. Croquet Balls (one for each player or team).
  3. Croquet Wickets (6-wicket or 9-wicket).
  4. Croquet Stakes
  5. Court
  6. Measuring Device(Optional)
  7. Croquet Pegs(Optional)
  8. Rules & Scorecards
